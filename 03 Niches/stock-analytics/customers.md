# customers: stock-analytics

## primary customer (v1)
financial newsletter operators with 5k–50k subscribers who need to cover 10–20 stocks per week. they have an audience and a publishing deadline. they will pay for structured signal context they can build their writing on top of. they are a business customer — less price-sensitive than retail.

## secondary customers
- self-directed retail investors who research their own picks but want a pre-earnings checklist they can trust
- small RIAs covering 20–40 client portfolios who want a weekly briefing they can personalize

## note on retail-first risk
pure retail is hard to monetize at $29–$79/month — they churn fast and conversion is slow. newsletter operators and small RIAs have a direct business reason to pay. start there.

## what they pay for today
- seeking alpha premium — opinion-heavy, not systematically signal-scored
- bloomberg or refinitiv — institutional pricing, more depth than needed
- manual research + fintwit — time-consuming, inconsistent

## what they actually need
- structured pre-earnings context for their specific watchlist
- consistent format so they can assess signal quality over time
- coverage of the stocks they actually write or talk about

## acquisition assumption
communities: substack finance writers, r/investing, x fintwit, seeking alpha commenters
give away one free earnings brief per week, convert on watchlist size and earnings alert coverage
