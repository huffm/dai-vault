# customers: kalshi-analytics

## primary customer (v1)
active kalshi traders who focus on economic data markets — people who trade CPI, NFP, and FOMC contracts regularly and are already doing manual probability research. they know CMEFedWatch exists; they don't have a brief that assembles it.

## secondary customers
- macro traders who use kalshi as a real-money hedge on economic outcomes (they have more WTP than pure prediction market hobbyists)
- quantitative forecasters who want a structured feed of kalshi price vs reference probability for their own models

## note on hobbyist forecasters
likely to use for free but unlikely to pay — deprioritize as a conversion target unless a community emerges organically

## what they pay for today
- nothing structured — most kalshi traders operate on gut and news consumption
- metaculus, manifold, or prediction tracker sites — aggregate probabilities, no actionable brief
- bloomberg or reuters — news, not market intelligence

## what they actually need
- a systematic view of which markets look mispriced and why
- consistent signal context so they can build confidence over time
- a way to prioritize attention across a large market catalog

## acquisition assumption
communities: r/predictit, kalshi discord, x prediction market accounts, forecasting communities
reach them with free daily market flags, convert with depth and alert tier
