# positioning: sports-analytics

## market reality
the incumbent products already cover the obvious surfaces:

- giant odds boards
- real-time line shopping
- sportsbook deep links
- positive EV feeds
- expert picks and tout-style content
- model builders and alert systems

trying to beat them at breadth would force us into a bigger, noisier product. that is the wrong fight for a solo founder.

## product category
this product is a **decision artifact**, not a picks feed.

one game produces one compact read:

- what changed in the market
- which signals agree
- which signals conflict
- how fresh the data is
- why this game deserves attention now, later, or not at all

the artifact is the product. the archive of artifacts is the trust system.

## core promise
compress 30-45 minutes of manual checking into a read that takes under 90 seconds to consume without hiding the reasoning.

## who this is for
serious recreational bettors who already do research, distrust generic picks, and want faster clarity without surrendering control.

## why current tools leave a gap
- **too much surface area:** users open five tabs and still have to synthesize the picture themselves
- **too many conclusions, not enough receipts:** picks are easy to publish and hard to trust
- **alerts arrive without context:** users know that something moved, but not whether it matters
- **good data is trapped in workflow friction:** the user spends effort navigating the tool instead of deciding on the game

## the wedge
we do not win by being bigger. we win by being easier to trust and easier to consume.

the wedge is:

- **transparent:** every read shows the signal table, timestamps, and conflicts
- **fast:** one artifact answers "is this game worth my attention?" quickly
- **auditable:** each read is saved with the line at dispatch and reviewed later against the closing market
- **calm:** fewer screens, fewer filters, less noise
- **designed:** the product should feel premium and intentional, not like a spreadsheet with logos

## product principles

### transparent
- show open line, current line, and time of last refresh
- show where signals disagree instead of forcing a fake conclusion
- show why confidence is high or low
- keep a visible audit trail of prior reads

### desirable
- the user should feel more in control after reading, not more dependent
- the product should reduce anxiety and tab-hopping
- every screen should feel like it respects the user's time

### useful
- every artifact must help the user judge whether the current number is still worth attention
- the product must be actionable in the sense of timing and context, even if it does not tell the user what side to bet

### easy to use
- one primary workflow per visit
- no giant dashboard required to get value
- the brief should be understandable in one pass on mobile

### attractive
- strong visual hierarchy
- compact but premium presentation
- confidence, movement, and disagreement should be scannable at a glance

## what v1 should be best at
- **speed to comprehension:** the fastest way to understand whether a game deserves attention
- **reasoning visibility:** the user can see why the artifact says what it says
- **accountability:** old reads do not disappear; they become history the product can be judged against

## what v1 should not try to be
- not an odds screen
- not a sportsbook router
- not a picks marketplace
- not a community feed
- not a props platform on day one
- not an everything-for-everyone betting app

## moat hypothesis
if the product consistently produces readable, timestamped, auditable artifacts, trust compounds over time.

that compounding trust is more defensible for us than trying to match incumbents feature-for-feature.
