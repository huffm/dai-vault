# landing page: sports-analytics

## purpose
the landing page should make the product feel like a serious multi-sport platform without promising more operational coverage than we can support well.

it should sell the product on:

- clarity
- trust
- speed to comprehension
- premium presentation

not on generic "AI picks" language.

## message hierarchy

### 1. hero
say what the product is in one line.

recommended direction:

**transparent pre-game reads before the line settles**

supporting subhead:

**for serious recreational bettors who want fast context, visible reasoning, and less noise across NFL, NBA, NCAAF, NCAAB, and MLB**

cta:

- primary: `open matchup analyzer`
- secondary: `see how a read works`

the left hero card should close with one compact orientation section that previews what a read surfaces. this should be subtle and useful, not a second hero.

### 2. why it is different
immediately distinguish the product from picks feeds and giant odds boards.

core line:

**not picks. not a giant dashboard. one compact decision artifact per game.**

this should read as an integrated product principle inside the differentiation section, not as a heavy full-width banner that interrupts the page rhythm.

### 3. what the artifact includes
show the anatomy of a read:

- market movement
- signal agreement
- signal conflict
- freshness
- confidence
- audit trail

### 4. why trust it
the trust layer needs to be explicit:

- timestamped reads
- visible reasoning
- archived history
- post-close market review

### 5. sport coverage
show breadth at the brand level, but separate that from live operational depth.

recommended structure:

- `live now:` NFL, NBA
- `next:` NCAAF, NCAAB
- `planned:` MLB

this gives platform credibility without creating a false quality claim.

### 6. workflow simplicity
explain that the product reduces tab-hopping and research sprawl.

core line:

**understand a game in under 90 seconds instead of checking five tools.**

### 7. final call to action
close with a focused next step:

- `run a matchup read`
- `get the weekly NFL read`
- `join the waitlist`

## visual direction

### overall feel
premium, modern, sharp, and calm.

the product should feel more like an editorial intelligence surface than a gambling dashboard.

### what to emphasize
- large, confident typography
- layered dark surfaces with strong depth
- precise accent color usage
- visual separation between live/next/planned sports
- cards that feel intentional, not utility-first and generic

### what to avoid
- purple-on-white startup look
- cluttered data-board presentation
- generic ai gradients without product meaning
- overuse of badges, charts, or fake metrics
- screaming sportsbook aesthetics

## page structure
1. hero with product promise and sample read preview
2. why it wins section with an integrated product-principle inset
3. artifact anatomy section
4. sport coverage section
5. trust and archive section
6. final cta section

## content rules
- lead with the user outcome, not the model
- use "read" or "decision artifact" more than "pick"
- show confidence and disagreement as product strengths
- keep the copy direct and low-hype
- if a section sounds like a generic ai betting site, rewrite it
