# customers: sports-analytics

## primary customer (v1)
recreational bettors who take it seriously — people betting $500–$5000/week who research their own picks but are frustrated with the tout model. they already pay for picks ($30–$100/month) and are skeptical. they want context to make their own decision, not someone else's opinion.

## secondary customers
- DFS players (daily fantasy) who need lineup context before lock
- sports media writers who want structured game previews without doing manual research

## what the primary customer is trying to do
- decide whether a game deserves attention before the market moves further
- understand the signal picture without opening five different tools
- keep control of the final decision instead of blindly tailing a pick feed
- build trust in a process over time, not just consume one-off recommendations

## what they pay for today
- tout pick services — low trust, no accountability, no reasoning shown
- beat writer subscriptions — qualitative and slow
- handicapping forums — inconsistent, time-consuming
- mature betting tools — strong data, but still require the user to do too much synthesis work

## what frustrates them
- too many alerts with too little explanation
- picks without receipts
- dashboards that make them work before they get value
- uncertainty about whether a line move actually matters
- tools that feel powerful but still leave them doing the hard part manually

## what they actually need
- a brief that shows the signal picture, not just a conclusion
- visible reasoning and timestamps, so they can judge freshness and trustworthiness
- consistent enough format that they can judge its track record over time
- delivered early enough to act before the line moves further
- a product that feels calm, fast, and premium rather than cluttered

## why they would switch
- they can understand one game in under 90 seconds
- they keep decision ownership instead of outsourcing judgment
- the archive lets them review whether the product's reads held up
- the format is simpler than a mature research suite but more trustworthy than a picks feed

## acquisition assumption
communities: r/sportsbook, x betting accounts, discord betting servers
free tier covers one NFL game brief per week — NFL is the sample sport because it is the most recognizable and highest-trust entry point
NBA requires starter or pro — used as a conversion lever for users who want year-round coverage
first paid conversion is someone already spending money on picks who wants something more transparent
