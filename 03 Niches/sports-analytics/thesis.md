# thesis: sports-analytics

## core problem
serious recreational bettors do not lack data. they lack fast, trustworthy synthesis.

lines open days before a game and move as information hits the market. by the time a user manually checks injuries, schedule spots, weather, and line movement across multiple tools, either the number has already changed or they still are not sure whether the game deserves attention.

## what we do
deliver a scored pre-game decision artifact before the line settles.

not picks. not a giant odds screen. a compact read that shows:

- what changed in the market
- which signals agree
- which signals conflict
- how confident the picture is
- why the current number may or may not reflect the full picture

## the wedge
the wedge is not more data. the wedge is **transparent synthesis with an audit trail**.

the product should be the fastest path from noisy pre-game data to a trustworthy read that can later be reviewed against the closing market.

NFL and NBA remain the first slice. NFL gives weekly, high-trust entry with manageable volume. NBA keeps the product active year-round and stress-tests the same pipeline with different signal patterns.

## why now
odds apis, injury feeds, and line movement data are accessible enough to assemble the picture. the gap is no longer raw access. the gap is speed to comprehension and confidence in the reasoning. agents can compress the synthesis step from manual research time into a short, repeatable workflow.

## what we are not
- we are not a tout service or pick-seller
- we are not a fantasy platform
- we are not an odds-board product
- we do not place bets or integrate with books in v1
- we are not trying to win on feature breadth against incumbent betting tools
