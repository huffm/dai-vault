# target customer: kalshi-v1

## primary
active Kalshi traders who trade economic data markets regularly — people who trade CPI, NFP, and FOMC contracts and already do manual probability research before events. they know CMEFedWatch exists. they don't have a brief that assembles it with Kalshi prices side by side.

## what they look like
- trade Kalshi at least weekly, focus on macro/economic events
- check BLS consensus estimates and Fed futures manually before sizing positions
- aware of the reference probability tools but assembling them takes time
- looking for an edge that is systematic, not just a gut read on the headline

## first conversion trigger
they receive a free pre-event brief before a CPI release, find it surfaces a divergence between the Kalshi price and the CME/consensus reference they hadn't checked that morning, and decide it's worth paying to have it assembled automatically for every event.

## secondary
- macro traders who use Kalshi contracts as a hedging layer on economic outcomes — higher WTP than hobbyist forecasters
- quantitative forecasters who want a structured feed of Kalshi price vs reference probability for their own models

## who this is not for
- hobbyist forecasters with no money on the line — unlikely to convert to paid
- prediction market newcomers — this product assumes they already know the platform
