# target customer: stock-v1

## primary
financial newsletter operators with 5k–50k subscribers who write about 10–20 stocks per week. they have a publishing deadline, an audience they are accountable to, and a direct business reason to pay for structured context. $149/month is within their tool budget.

## what they look like
- publish on Substack, Beehiiv, or their own platform
- cover earnings events regularly and need to be prepared
- do their own analysis but want a signal checklist before they commit to a take
- currently spend 1-2 hours manually pulling context per stock before earnings

## first conversion trigger
they use the free earnings brief for one stock they were planning to cover, find it surfaces something they would have missed (estimate revision, insider sale, technical divergence), and decide it saves them enough prep time to be worth paying for.

## secondary
- small RIAs covering 20–40 client portfolios who want a weekly brief they can reference before client calls
- self-directed retail investors who research seriously and want a pre-earnings checklist

## note on retail prioritization
retail is the largest group but the hardest to convert at $29–$149/month. target newsletter operators and RIAs first — they have professional accountability that makes the brief immediately valuable.

## who this is not for
- passive index investors
- institutional desks with Bloomberg access and in-house analysts
